Build "Meridian", an analytics dashboard over global CO2 emissions by country and year. The data is a local JSON file with, per country, a yearly series of total CO2 (million tonnes), CO2 per capita (tonnes), year-over-year change, population, and a region. Load it locally, with no runtime network.

A left sidebar switches two views, "Overview" and "Explorer". Global controls sit atop both: a Region filter (starting on "All regions", also listing each continent), a Year selector (defaulting to the most recent year), and a Metric toggle ("Total" and "Per capita").

Overview shows four KPI cards (total emissions, average per capita, top emitter, and year-over-year change for the selected year) above two charts: a line chart of the chosen metric over time for one country, with a small "Country" picker; and a horizontal bar chart ranking the top countries that year. Render the charts as static SVG so they look identical every load.

Explorer shows a sortable table of every country for the selected year (columns: rank, country, region, and the emissions metrics). Clicking a header sorts by it; clicking again flips direction. A search box filters by country name, the region dropdown narrows to one continent, and a live result count (such as "42 countries") tracks the filters. When nothing matches, show an empty-state message instead of an empty table; a "Clear filters" button restores the full set.

On first load, open Overview with "All regions", the latest year, "Total", and the United States plotted. Make it polished and modern, with clean typography and a responsive layout.
