I want to build a Financial Stability Board (FSB) website. When someone opens the site, they land on the homepage that displays the latest news and featured reports on financial stability topics. The homepage has a main navigation menu at the top with five primary sections: "ABOUT THE FSB", "WORK OF THE FSB", "PUBLICATIONS", "CONSULTATIONS", and "PRESS".

If I click on "ABOUT THE FSB" in the navigation menu, I'm taken to the About the FSB page that explains the FSB's mandate and mission as an international body that monitors and makes recommendations about the global financial system. This page describes how the FSB promotes international financial stability by coordinating national financial authorities and international standard-setting bodies as they work toward developing strong regulatory, supervisory and other financial sector policies. The page details the mandate of the FSB and the organizational framework including the Plenary, Steering Committee, and three Standing Committees.

From the homepage, if I click on "PUBLICATIONS" in the main menu, it takes me to the Publications page where I can browse through all FSB policy documents, reports, and evaluations. This page shows 914 results with filtering options on the left sidebar. I can filter content by search keyword, content types, policy areas (such as "Climate-related Risks", "Cyber Resilience", "Crypto Assets", and "Nonbank Financial Intermediation"), and by date range using "Published After" and "Published Before" fields. The results are paginated across 92 pages, and each publication listing is clickable and shows the title, date, and a brief description.

If I'm interested in a specific policy area like climate change, I can navigate from the homepage by clicking "WORK OF THE FSB" in the main menu, which takes me to a page showing different policy areas. From there, clicking on "Financial Innovation and Structural Change" under the work areas, or navigating through the breadcrumb path "Work of the FSB > Financial Innovation and Structural Change > Climate-related Risks", takes me to the Climate-related Risks page. This page explains that the FSB is coordinating internationally the work to address climate-related financial risks, and covers four main collapsible sections: Disclosures, Data, Vulnerability Analysis, and Regulatory and supervisory practices and tools. The page also includes a roadmap diagram and access to key documents including progress reports and TCFD Reports.

From the homepage, clicking on "CONSULTATIONS" in the menu takes me to the Consultations page where I can see both current and past public consultations. The page explains that the FSB issues consultations for all policy documents and describes the three transparent steps of the consultation process: consultations generally last 60 days, public responses are published within 15 days of closure, and overview reports are published when final policy documents are approved. The page displays three sections with images: "Current consultations" to find out what the FSB currently consults on, "Past consultations" to browse all public consultations the FSB has conducted, and "Responses to past consultations" where responses received to FSB consultations are available publicly.

If I want to see press materials, I can click "PRESS" in the main navigation menu from any page, which takes me to the Press page. This page organizes 1034 press items including press releases, speeches and statements, and other press materials. The page has filters on the left sidebar including search, policy areas, and date range filters ("Published After" and "Published Before"). The results are paginated with pagination controls at the bottom showing page numbers and a "Next" button, and the latest updates are featured prominently at the top with dates displayed.

All pages include the same main navigation menu at the top with five sections: "ABOUT THE FSB", "WORK OF THE FSB", "PUBLICATIONS", "CONSULTATIONS", and "PRESS", allowing me to navigate between these sections at any time. Each page also includes a breadcrumb navigation below the main menu showing the current page location (for example: "Home > About the FSB" or "Home > Work of the FSB > Financial Innovation and Structural Change > Climate-related Risks"). The homepage also includes three clickable sections at the bottom: "Data", "Video and Audio", and "Organisation and Members", which provide access to additional resources.
